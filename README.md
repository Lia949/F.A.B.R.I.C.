# F.A.B.R.I.C.
This is the official repository for F.A.B.R.I.C., an open-source Pen and Paper ruleset. The aim of this project is to provide a solid framework to create your own world - including magic, technology and so on - and easily adapt these provided rules so that they reflect the heart of your creation.

## Why do we need yet another Pen and Paper ruleset?
There are hundreds, if not thousands, different rules for Pen and Paper out there. So why do we need another one?  

The answer is simple. There is no system with free magic. Let me explain.
While many of them are great rules in general, there is one thing all of them seem to share: magic only consists of a list of predefined spells. While it is fun to explore creative ways to solve a problem using these spells, they ultimately represent a rigid tool.
Meanwhile in (some) Fantasy books we read about the exploration of magic, the possibilities and creativity characters encounter while they form their own path of magic. They connect to their power, they feel the magic. And they decide what to do with it.   

That is how the idea of F.A.B.R.I.C was born. Rules that allow a caster to extend their magical sense and change reality to their imagination. Not bound to one specific world or even genre. It shouldn't matter if you want to play in a classical high-magic fantasy world or in a setting where magic is rare and dangerous. Even hacking in a science fiction setting can follow these rules and thus make programmers the coolest class to play. This is why the magic rules are only a framework (*fundamental structure used as the basis for something else*) that should be used to create your own **magic system**.

Of course there is no sense in having only rules for magic and nothing else. This is the reason why I created a full ruleset. I tried to be as simple as possible while maintaining a comfortable realism (especially for combat) and adaptability to different genres. And because I'm a nerd it is based on Binomial distributions[^1].

## Playtesting
In the folder settings are different rule systems that are built on top of the basic framework. They come together with a oneshot that can be used for testing. 

## Contribution
You might see why the goal of creating these rules is a rather complex problem to tackle. After chewing on it for almost five years on my own and being unsatisfied with the result, some friends convinced me to put it out there and let the hivemind of nerds work on it.
I will be happy about every single person that gives these rules a shot and simply report back what they like or not. And if you want to contribute more, please get in contact!
